Chapter One: The Crimson Contract

Experience the opening chapter of "Doraleous and Associates: A Tale of Glory" and discover why readers can't put this epic fantasy adventure down.

by Brian M. Shoemaker

Chapter 1 The Crimson Contract ~15 minute read

The tavern's smoky air carried the familiar scents of ale, sweat, and secrets‚Äîthe holy trinity of any establishment where sellswords gathered to ply their trade. Doraleous sat in his customary corner booth, back to the wall, eyes scanning the room with the practiced ease of someone who had survived too many attempted assassinations to count. The Gilded Griffin wasn't the finest tavern in Aldermere, but it served his purposes: discrete, defensible, and populated by the sort of people who minded their own business.

"You're brooding again," Zara observed, sliding into the seat across from him with her usual grace. The mage's violet eyes sparkled with amusement as she gestured for the barmaid. "It's that particular expression you get when you're trying to decide whether a potential client is worth the trouble they're likely to cause."

Doraleous raised an eyebrow. "And what makes you think I'm considering taking on a client? Perhaps I'm simply enjoying the ambiance of our fine establishment." He gestured broadly at the tavern's interior, where a trio of mercenaries were arm-wrestling while their companions placed increasingly reckless bets.

"Because," Zara said, accepting a goblet of wine from the serving girl, "Gareth has been standing guard outside for the past hour, and you only post our stalwart defender when you're expecting someone important. Also, you've checked your pocket watch seven times in the last ten minutes."

"Observant as always." Doraleous couldn't suppress a slight smile. After three years of working together, his associates had learned to read him perhaps too well. "Lady Valeria Ashcroft sent word yesterday. She requires our services for what she described as 'a matter of utmost delicacy and substantial remuneration.'"

Zara's expression shifted to one of interest mixed with caution. "The Duke's daughter? That's either very good news or very bad news. There's rarely anything in between with nobility."

"My thoughts exactly." Doraleous reached into his coat and withdrew a sealed letter bearing the distinctive crimson wax seal of House Ashcroft. "She's requesting a meeting tonight to discuss the details. Given her family's... reputation for political maneuvering, I suspect this won't be a simple escort mission or bandit clearing."

The tavern's front door opened with a creak that somehow managed to cut through the ambient noise. A figure in a deep blue hooded cloak entered, moving with the fluid confidence of someone accustomed to command. Several patrons glanced up, then quickly returned to their drinks‚Äîa sure sign that they recognized nobility when they saw it, even disguised.

Lady Valeria Ashcroft approached their table with purposeful strides, her hood casting shadows across features that were striking rather than conventionally beautiful. Intelligence glittered in her green eyes as she took in the tavern's occupants, no doubt cataloguing potential threats with the same thoroughness that Doraleous employed.

"Master Doraleous," she said, her voice carrying the crisp authority of someone raised to rule. "Thank you for agreeing to meet on such short notice."

"My lady," Doraleous rose and gestured to an empty chair. "Please, join us. May I present my associate, Zara Nightwhisper, our resident expert in matters arcane and ancient."

Valeria's gaze lingered on Zara for a moment, taking in the subtle signs that marked her as a practitioner of the magical arts‚Äîthe faint luminescence in her eyes, the way the air seemed to shimmer slightly around her fingers, the small crystal pendant that hummed with contained energy.

"A pleasure," Valeria said, settling into the offered chair. "Your reputation precedes you, Lady Nightwhisper. I understand you were once affiliated with the Celestial Academy before... pursuing independent ventures."

Zara's smile was polite but guarded. "Academic politics can be quite stifling. I found that real-world application of magical theory provided better learning opportunities."

"Indeed." Valeria turned her attention back to Doraleous. "I trust this location provides sufficient privacy for sensitive discussion?"

"The Griffin has served us well for such purposes," Doraleous confirmed. "The proprietor ensures that certain conversations go unheard, and my associate Gareth is positioned to discourage eavesdropping. You may speak freely."

Valeria nodded and leaned forward slightly, her voice dropping to barely above a whisper. "Three days ago, something was stolen from my father's private collection. Not gold or jewels‚Äîthose we could replace. What was taken is far more valuable and infinitely more dangerous."

"What manner of item?" Zara asked, her scholarly instincts immediately engaged.

"An artifact from the Age of Shadows," Valeria replied, watching their faces carefully. "The Crown of Whispers‚Äîa relic that was supposedly destroyed along with the Shadow Empire a thousand years ago."

The temperature at the table seemed to drop several degrees. Doraleous felt the familiar chill that accompanied mentions of the Shadow Empire, that dark period in history when malevolent sorcerers had nearly consumed the world. Beside him, Zara had gone very still, her wine goblet forgotten in her hand.

"That's impossible," Zara said finally. "The Crown of Whispers was destroyed by Archmage Lumineth herself during the final battle at Shadowfall. I've read the historical accounts‚Äîmultiple sources confirm its destruction."

"So we all believed," Valeria said grimly. "Yet my father possessed what he claimed was the genuine article, hidden in a vault beneath our estate for the past thirty years. Whether it's real or an elaborate fake matters less than the fact that someone was willing to kill three guards and a house mage to obtain it."

Doraleous leaned back in his chair, processing the implications. "You want us to retrieve it."

"I want you to find it and either return it to my father's vault or destroy it permanently," Valeria corrected. "If the Crown truly possesses the power attributed to it in the ancient texts, then its very existence poses a threat to the stability of the realm."

"And if we refuse?" Doraleous asked, though he suspected he already knew the answer.

Valeria's expression hardened. "Then I will find others to undertake the task. But given your reputation for both discretion and success in matters involving dangerous magical artifacts, I would prefer to work with professionals I can trust."

"The pay?" Zara inquired with characteristic directness.

"Fifty thousand gold crowns upon successful completion," Valeria said without hesitation. "Half again if you can accomplish the task within a fortnight."

It was an impressive sum‚Äîenough to fund their operation for two years or allow each of them to retire comfortably if they chose. Doraleous found himself studying Lady Valeria's face, searching for signs of deception or hidden motivations. What he saw was determination mixed with barely controlled fear.

"You believe the thief intends to use the Crown," he said. It wasn't a question.

"I believe," Valeria said carefully, "that anyone willing to commit murder to obtain a Shadow Empire artifact has purposes that extend far beyond mere profit. The Crown of Whispers, if the legends are true, grants its wearer the ability to command shadow magic and influence the minds of others. In the wrong hands..."

She didn't need to finish the sentence. The implications were clear enough. Doraleous exchanged a glance with Zara, seeing his own thoughts reflected in her expression. This was exactly the sort of high-stakes, world-threatening mission that their little company seemed to attract with disturbing regularity.

"We'll need details," Doraleous said finally. "Everything you know about the theft, the artifact's history, and any leads you might have regarding the identity of the thief."

Valeria reached into her cloak and withdrew a leather portfolio sealed with the same crimson wax as her letter. "Everything my father was willing to share is contained within. I should warn you‚Äîsome of the information contradicts accepted historical accounts. My father has access to... alternative sources."

"Alternative sources?" Zara's academic curiosity was clearly piqued.

"Duke Ashcroft has long been a patron of researchers who investigate topics that the official histories prefer to ignore," Valeria explained. "He believes that understanding the past‚Äîall of it, not just the sanitized versions taught in schools‚Äîis essential to preventing its repetition."

Doraleous accepted the portfolio, feeling its weight both literal and figurative. "We'll need time to review this information and consult with our third associate. Gareth's insights have proven invaluable in past endeavors."

"Of course. I'll await your decision." Valeria rose gracefully from her chair. "But please don't take too long. Every day that passes increases the likelihood that whoever stole the Crown will discover how to use it."

As Lady Valeria made her way toward the tavern's exit, Doraleous found himself staring at the portfolio in his hands. Something about this entire situation felt orchestrated, as if events were falling into place according to some grand design that he couldn't quite perceive.

"Well," Zara said, breaking the contemplative silence, "it seems we're about to embark on another adventure that will likely test the limits of our sanity and survival instincts."

"Most likely," Doraleous agreed, tucking the portfolio inside his coat. "Though I suspect this particular adventure may test something far more fundamental than our survival skills."

"Such as?"

Doraleous met his friend's questioning gaze with a wry smile. "Our ability to save the world without destroying it in the process."

End of Chapter One
